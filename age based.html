Problems on Ages
UAT Exam Preparation | Quantitative Reasoning
Hot Topic Appears in every last year UAT exam

Problems on Ages questions involve past, present, and future time periods. Once you understand how ages change over time → these questions become very easy to solve.

What Are Age Problems?

Age problems ask you to determine the age of one or more people using clues about:

  • Present age → current age of a person
  • Past age → age at some earlier time
  • Future age → age at some later time
  • Age difference → gap between two people's ages
  • Ratio of ages → proportional relationship between ages
  • Sum of ages → total when ages are added together

Translate given clues into equations and solve for unknown ages.

Key Formulas
Present age → Future age
$y = x + n$
where $x$ = present age, $n$ = years later, and $y$ = future age
Present age → Past age
$z = x - m$
where $x$ = present age, $m$ = years ago, and $z$ = past age
Age difference (constant)
$A - B = A' - B'$
where $A, B$ = present ages, $A', B'$ = future ages

Core Concept

  • People grow older at the same rate
  • Every year → everyone becomes 1 year older
  • The difference between two people's ages never changes
Key Rule
$A_{\text{present}} - B_{\text{present}} = A_{\text{future}} - B_{\text{future}}$
where $A_{\text{present}}$ = present age of person A, $B_{\text{present}}$ = present age of person B
The age difference remains constant over time
Example
A is 20 years old B is 15 years old
Current difference: $20 - 15 = 5$
After 10 years: A = 30, B = 25
New difference: $30 - 25 = 5$
The age difference remains constant

Problems on Ages
Important Terminology and Concepts

Understanding these terms makes solving questions much easier.

1. Present Age

The current age of a person.

Example

If Abel is 18 years old today → Present age = 18 years

2. Age After n Years

Age after $n$ years = Present age + $n$

Example

Current age = 20, after 6 years = $20 + 6 = 26$

3. Age Before n Years

Age before $n$ years = Present age − $n$

Example

Current age = 25, 7 years ago = $25 - 7 = 18$

4. Age Ratio

A ratio compares the ages of two or more people.

Example

A = 24, B = 30 → $24 : 30 = 4 : 5$

Converting Ratios

Ratio = 3 : 5 → A = $3x$, B = $5x$

5. Constant Age Difference

The difference between two people's ages never changes.

$A - B = A' - B'$
difference remains constant
Example

A = 40, B = 30 → $40 - 30 = 10$
After 15 years: 55 − 45 = 10

The difference stays the same forever

6. Linear Equations

Example

Abel is 4 years older than Biruk.
Let Biruk = $x$ → Abel = $x + 4$

7. Sum of Ages

Example

Sum of A and B = 40
If A = $x$ → B = $40 - x$

Key Takeaway: Almost every age problem becomes one or two simple equations

Types of Problems and Important Formulas

Types of Age Problems

Type 1: Direct Age Questions

Find someone's present, past, or future age.

Type 2: Comparative Age Questions

Compare ages of two people (e.g., A is 6 years older than B, A is twice as old as B).

Type 3: Ratio Problems

Ages given in ratios (e.g., present ratio = 4:5, after 5 years = 5:6).

Type 4: Age Puzzle Questions

Involve several people and multiple time conditions.

Type 5: Mixed Problems

Age concepts combined with averages, percentages, or arithmetic.

Important Formulas on Problems on Ages

Formula 1
$n \times x$
If current age is $x$, then $n$ times the age is $nx$.
Example: Age = 8, 3 times = $3 \times 8 = 24$ years
Formula 2
$x + n$
Age $n$ years later/hence = $x + n$.
Example: Age = 15, after 7 years = $15 + 7 = 22$ years
Formula 3
$x - n$
Age $n$ years ago = $x - n$.
Example: Age = 30, 12 years ago = $30 - 12 = 18$ years
Formula 4
$a : b = ax : bx$
Ages in ratio $a : b$ will be $ax$ and $bx$.
Example: Ratio 3:5 → ages = $3x$ and $5x$ years
Formula 5
$\frac{x}{n}$
$\frac{1}{n}$ of the age = $\frac{x}{n}$.
Example: Age = 24, $\frac{1}{3}$ of age = $\frac{24}{3} = 8$ years

Solutions

S1 Answer: A. 2 times
Solution:

Let Dawit's present age = $x$

Father's present age = $x + 3x = 4x$

After 8 years:
$4x + 8 = \frac{5}{2}(x + 8)$

$2(4x + 8) = 5(x + 8)$

$8x + 16 = 5x + 40$

$3x = 24$

$x = 8$

After further 8 years (16 years from now):
Father's age = $4(8) + 16 = 48$

Dawit's age = $8 + 16 = 24$

Required ratio = $\frac{48}{24} = 2$
S2 Answer: A. 4 years
Solution:

Let the ages of children be:
$x, x+3, x+6, x+9, x+12$

Sum of ages = 50

$x + (x+3) + (x+6) + (x+9) + (x+12) = 50$

$5x + 30 = 50$

$5x = 20$

$x = 4$

Age of the youngest child = 4 years
S3 Answer: A. 14 years
Solution:

Let the son's present age = $x$

At the time of son's birth, father's age = $38 - x$

According to the statement:
$38 - x = x$

$2x = 38$

$x = 19$

Son's age 5 years back = $19 - 5 = 14$ years
S4 Answer: D. 10 years
Solution:

Let C's age = $x$

B's age = $2x$

A's age = $2x + 2$

Sum of ages = 27

$(2x + 2) + 2x + x = 27$

$5x + 2 = 27$

$5x = 25$

$x = 5$

B's age = $2(5) = 10$ years
S5 Answer: A. 24 years
Solution:

Let Abel's present age = $5x$
Hana's present age = $4x$

After 3 years:
$\frac{5x+3}{4x+3} = \frac{11}{9}$

$9(5x+3) = 11(4x+3)$

$45x + 27 = 44x + 33$

$x = 6$

Hana's present age = $4(6) = 24$ years
S6 Answer: D. 22 years
Solution:

Let son's present age = $x$

Man's present age = $x + 24$

After 2 years:
$(x+24) + 2 = 2(x+2)$

$x + 26 = 2x + 4$

$x = 22$ years
S7 Answer: A. 16 years
Solution:

Let ages of Selam and Hana 6 years ago be $6x$ and $5x$

Present ages:
Selam = $6x + 6$
Hana = $5x + 6$

Four years hence:
$\frac{6x+10}{5x+10} = \frac{11}{10}$

$10(6x+10) = 11(5x+10)$

$60x + 100 = 55x + 110$

$5x = 10$

$x = 2$

Hana's present age = $5(2) + 6 = 16$ years
S8 Answer: D. 20 years
Solution:

Let son's present age = $x$
Father's present age = $60 - x$

Six years ago:
$(60-x) - 6 = 5(x-6)$

$54 - x = 5x - 30$

$6x = 84$

$x = 14$

Son's age after 6 years = $14 + 6 = 20$ years
S9 Answer: B. 15 years
Solution:

Let Dawit's present age = $4x$
Selam's present age = $3x$

After 6 years:
$4x + 6 = 26$

$4x = 20$

$x = 5$

Selam's present age = $3(5) = 15$ years
S10 Answer: D. 24.5 years
Solution:

Let Selam's age = $x$
Dawit's age = $x - 7$

According to the ratio:
$\frac{x-7}{x} = \frac{7}{9}$

$9(x-7) = 7x$

$9x - 63 = 7x$

$2x = 63$

$x = 31.5$

Dawit's age = $31.5 - 7 = 24.5$ years
